Pregnancy

Signs That Your Pregnancy Needs Immediate Attention

Most pregnancy discomforts are normal, but some symptoms mean you should call or visit your doctor without delay.

Severe abdominal pain or cramps

Mild stretching pains are common, but strong, one‑sided or persistent abdominal pain can be a warning sign. If the pain is sudden, associated with bleeding, dizziness or fainting, seek emergency care.

Bleeding or fluid leakage

Light spotting can occur, but heavy bleeding, clots or a sudden gush or continuous leakage of fluid from the vagina should be checked immediately, especially after the first trimester.

Reduced baby movements

After about 28 weeks, you get used to your baby’s pattern. If you feel a clear reduction in movements, drink some water, rest on your left side and focus. If movements are still less, contact your doctor or visit the hospital.

Severe headache, blurred vision or swelling

New‑onset severe headaches, blurring of vision, flashing lights, or sudden swelling of the face and hands can suggest high blood pressure in pregnancy and need urgent review.

When you are unsure, it is always safer to speak to your doctor. At Mother’s Infertility & IVF Centre, you are encouraged to call for guidance rather than ignore worrying symptoms.
