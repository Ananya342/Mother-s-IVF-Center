IVF & Fertility

Understanding Your IVF Timeline Step by Step

A simple, week‑by‑week overview of what usually happens during an IVF cycle at Mother’s Infertility & IVF Centre.

1. Preparation & basic tests

Before starting IVF, Dr. Manisha Jain reviews your history, does basic blood tests and ultrasound scans, and may advise lifestyle changes. This helps your body respond better to treatment and keeps the plan safe for you.

2. Ovarian stimulation (10–12 days)

You take daily hormone injections to gently stimulate the ovaries to grow multiple eggs. You will have 2–3 scans to track the follicles, and your dose may be adjusted as needed.

3. Egg pickup & sperm collection

When the follicles are ready, a short procedure is done to collect the eggs. On the same day, a semen sample is collected and prepared in the andrology lab.

4. Fertilisation & embryo growth

In the embryology lab, IVF or ICSI is performed. The embryos are carefully monitored over 3–5 days and only good‑quality embryos are selected for transfer or freezing.

5. Embryo transfer & the two‑week wait

A selected embryo is placed in the uterus using a fine soft catheter. You can go home shortly after the procedure and continue your regular medicines while waiting for the pregnancy test.

Every couple’s situation is unique. At Mother’s Infertility & IVF Centre, your plan is personalised and explained in simple language at each step.
