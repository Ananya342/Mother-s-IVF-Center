PCOS, Lifestyle, and Fertility – What Really Helps?

PCOS is common and manageable. Small, steady lifestyle changes often make a real difference to periods, weight and fertility.

Focus on balanced, regular meals

Instead of strict “PCOS diets”, aim for regular meals with vegetables, whole grains, pulses and good‑quality protein. Limiting sugary drinks, packaged snacks and white flour helps keep insulin and weight under control.

Move your body most days

Brisk walking, cycling, light strength training or even dancing at home can help. Around 150 minutes of moderate activity per week is a realistic goal for most women.

Sleep and stress matter too

Poor sleep and constant stress can worsen hormonal imbalance. Simple breathing exercises, screen‑free wind‑down time and going to bed at a similar hour each night are small habits that add up.

When to seek medical help

If your periods are very irregular, you are trying to conceive, or you have symptoms like excessive hair growth or acne, you should meet a gynecologist or infertility specialist.

At Mother’s Infertility & IVF Centre, PCOS treatment plans combine lifestyle guidance with medicines only when needed, so that your long‑term health and fertility are both protected.
